End-to-End Workflow
From Data to Decision
1

Data Ingestion

ERP / GL data loaded into fact_financials. 36 months of history across 5 dimensions, 38 accounts, 5 BUs. MTD atomic grain with actual, budget, forecast, and prior year amounts.

2

Engine Run (Process A)

Pure math: raw variance at all intersections, netting detection, trend detection, materiality filter, decomposition. ~15 seconds, zero LLM cost. Produces ~10,700 material variances per period.

3

Engine Run (Process B)

Intelligence + LLM: cross-period correlation analysis (persistent, lead-lag, YoY), then 4-stage narrative generation (leaves → parents → sections → executive). ~30 min with GPT-4.1 Mini at concurrency 25.

4

AI Draft Generated

Every material variance gets 4 narrative levels: detail (analyst), midlevel (BU leader), summary (CFO), oneliner (dashboard). Status: AI_DRAFT. Each narrative includes decomposition drivers, correlation context, trend patterns, and cross-period intelligence.

5

Review Queue (Analyst Workflow)

Analysts review AI drafts in their daily landing page. Edit narratives, provide hypothesis feedback (thumbs up/down), add context. SLA tracking: green <12h, amber <24h, red >48h. Batch actions for efficiency. Status: ANALYST_REVIEWED.

6

Approval (Director / CFO)

Directors bulk-approve reviewed narratives. Report gate: only APPROVED content enters distributed reports. Rejected items return to review queue. Status: APPROVED.

↻ RAG Learning Loop: Approved narratives feed the knowledge base. Vector embeddings stored for retrieval. Future AI drafts use 2-3 similar approved commentaries as few-shot examples. Each approval cycle improves quality.
7

Report Distribution

Automated generation of Executive Flash (1-page PDF), Period-End Package (4-page), Board Narrative (letterhead), and XLSX with narrative tabs. Distributed via Teams, Slack, or email on schedule.

8

Next Period

Engine accumulates history. Cross-period analysis deepens: 3-month persistent streaks become 6-month structural patterns. Lead-lag chains extend. YoY echoes emerge. The system gets smarter with every period processed.

Narrative Pyramid
Four Levels of Financial Intelligence

Click each level to see an example narrative. Each level synthesizes from the level below + enriches with cross-cutting intelligence.

Board Level

On-demand | Approval-gated | 1-2 sentences

"Q2 underperformance driven by structural APAC volume softness persisting since March, partially offset by favorable OpEx timing. EBITDA margin compression of 180bps warrants strategic review of Cloud Services pricing model."

Summary (CFO)

Approved narratives only | Key metrics + root cause | 2-3 sentences

"Revenue $800K below Budget (-5.2%), marking the third consecutive month of underperformance. EBITDA compressed to 18.3% from budgeted 20.1%. Key driver: Cloud Services volume decline in APAC, a year-over-year echo of the June 2025 pattern."

Midlevel (BU Leader)

Own BU only | Reviewed + Approved | Drivers + trends

"Revenue in BU-East declined 5.2% vs Budget, driven primarily by Cloud Services (-$500K, volume-driven) and Licensing (-$200K, renewal timing). This represents a persistent 4-month pattern with cumulative impact of $2.3M. Travel expense offset partially with $45K favorable. 3 of 8 revenue lines contributed positively."

Detail (Analyst)

Full access | All statuses | Decomposition + correlations + 15 intelligence dimensions

"Cloud Services Revenue decreased by $500,000 (-8.2%) vs Budget. Unfavorable. Decomposition: Volume -$380K (unit sales 12% below plan), Price +$30K (2% price increase effective Q2), Mix -$120K (shift from Enterprise to SMB), FX -$30K (AUD weakness). Correlated with: APAC headcount increase in May (lead-lag, Confidence: 0.91). Trending unfavorable for 4 consecutive months (cumulative $1.8M). YoY echo: same pattern in June 2025 ($310K unfavorable). Risk: High. Peer comparison: BU-West Cloud Services is +3.2%, suggesting BU-East-specific operational issue. [AI Draft]"
